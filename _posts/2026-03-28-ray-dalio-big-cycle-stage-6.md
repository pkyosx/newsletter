## Summary

Billionaire investor Ray Dalio has officially declared that the post-1945 world order is over. In February 2026, following the Munich Security Conference, Dalio announced that the United States — and the world — has entered **Stage 6** of his "Big Cycle" framework: the phase of great disorder, where rules collapse and raw power decides outcomes. A viral Chinese-language video by 腦洞烏托邦 (Brainhole Utopia) brought this thesis to millions of viewers, laying out five pieces of evidence for why 2026 mirrors 1936 — the calm before the storm of World War II.

Here is what Dalio's framework says, why the parallels are alarming, and what it means for your money and your worldview.

## What Is the Big Cycle?

Ray Dalio, founder of Bridgewater Associates — the world's largest hedge fund — spent over a decade studying **500 years of rise-and-fall patterns** across the world's dominant empires: the Dutch, British, American, and Chinese orders. His conclusion: all monetary orders, political orders, and geopolitical orders follow a repeating pattern called the **Big Cycle**, typically lasting 75 to 150 years.

In his 2021 book *Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order*, Dalio outlined **six stages** of this cycle:

| Stage | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **1** | The New Order | A new power consolidates after conflict. Debt is low, cooperation is high. |
| **2** | Rise & Prosperity | Innovation, education, and exports drive growth. The empire becomes dominant. |
| **3** | The Bubble | Wealth inequality grows, debt balloons, speculation runs rampant. |
| **4** | Decline Begins | Unsustainable debt strains the economy. Productivity slows. |
| **5** | Pre-Breakdown | Internal divisions deepen. Populism surges. External challengers emerge. |
| **6** | Great Disorder | The old order collapses. Rules give way to power. Conflict — or transformation. |

The current American-led order began after 1945. By Dalio's clock, we are roughly 80 years in — right on schedule for the cycle's most dangerous phase.

## The Munich Moment: Stage 6 Is Official

On **February 18, 2026**, at the Munich Security Conference, world leaders effectively confirmed Dalio's thesis from the podium:

- **German Chancellor Friedrich Merz**: *"The world order as it has stood for decades no longer exists."*
- **French President Emmanuel Macron**: *Europe must "prepare for war."*
- **U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio**: *"We are in a new geopolitics era because the old world is gone."*

This was the moment Dalio moved his assessment from Stage 5 ("pre-breakdown") to **Stage 6** ("great disorder"). It was the first time in his career that he made this call explicitly. The international rules-based system — the UN, WTO, NATO consensus — is no longer the operating framework. In its place: **"might makes right."**

## Five Parallels Between 2026 and 1936

The viral video by 腦洞烏托邦 distilled Dalio's framework into five stark parallels between our present moment and the pre-war year of 1936:

### 1. Crushing Debt and Money Printing

In 1936, governments worldwide were still running massive deficits from Depression-era stimulus. Today, U.S. national debt stands at **$37 trillion**, with annual interest payments exceeding **$1 trillion** — more than the entire defense budget. When governments cannot repay debt honestly, Dalio warns, they print money, devalue currency, and erode the savings of ordinary citizens. This is not a hypothetical. It is happening.

### 2. Extreme Wealth Inequality

In the 1930s, the gap between the rich and the working class fueled the rise of populism on both the left and right — communism in the Soviet Union, fascism in Germany, Italy, and Japan. Today, the **top 1% of Americans hold 32% of national wealth**, while real wages for the bottom half have barely moved in decades. Dalio identifies two populist forces in the U.S. — **MAGA and WOKE** — as symptoms of the same underlying fracture: a society that no longer agrees on basic values or facts.

### 3. A Rising Challenger Power

In the 1930s, Germany and Japan rose to challenge the British-led order. Today, **China** is the rising power. It leads in 5G, electric vehicles, battery technology, solar manufacturing, and increasingly in AI. The U.S.-China relationship has moved from competition to confrontation: tech bans, chip export controls, and military posturing over Taiwan mirror the economic decoupling that preceded past great-power conflicts.

### 4. The Five Wars Are Active Simultaneously

Dalio identifies five types of "war" that escalate before outright military conflict. **All five are now active:**

1. **Trade War** — Tariffs on Chinese imports projected at 60%, with retaliatory measures from Beijing.
2. **Technology War** — Semiconductor export bans, AI restrictions, and TikTok battles.
3. **Capital War** — Debt devaluation through aggressive money printing; weaponization of the dollar system.
4. **Sanctions & Asset Seizures** — Russia's frozen $300B in reserves showed that dollar-denominated assets can be confiscated.
5. **Geopolitical/Military Tensions** — Taiwan remains the most dangerous flashpoint since the Cuban Missile Crisis.

In the 1930s, these wars escalated sequentially. Today, they are running in parallel — compressing the timeline.

### 5. Democracies Under Stress

In the 1930s, four democracies — Germany, Japan, Italy, and Spain — transitioned to autocracies during economic and social upheaval. Today, democratic backsliding is measurable: Freedom House has documented **18 consecutive years of global democratic decline**. Institutions designed for consensus are struggling with polarization, disinformation, and the speed of modern crises.

## What Comes After Stage 6?

Dalio is not a doomsayer by profession — he is a macro investor who bets real money on his models. His message is not that collapse is inevitable, but that **the pattern is clear and the risks are underpriced**.

Historically, Stage 6 transitions to a **new Stage 1** — a rebuilt order. After the 1940s, the Bretton Woods system, the United Nations, and American hegemony created 80 years of relative stability. The question is whether the transition this time involves catastrophic conflict or managed restructuring.

Dalio sees a narrow path to the latter: leaders who choose **"win-win" cooperation** over zero-sum power grabs. But he is not optimistic about human nature delivering that outcome.

## What It Means for You

Dalio's practical recommendations align with what serious macro investors are already doing:

- **Diversify globally.** Don't concentrate assets in a single country or currency. The risk of capital controls and currency devaluation is rising.
- **Hold hard assets.** Gold has surged as central banks worldwide increase reserves — a classic Stage 5-6 signal. Dalio has long advocated gold as a hedge against monetary disorder.
- **Reduce personal debt.** In a debt-devaluation cycle, being a borrower is a bet on inflation — which may work, but the volatility is brutal.
- **Stay informed, not paralyzed.** Understanding the cycle does not mean predicting the exact timing of crisis. It means positioning so you survive multiple scenarios.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The post-1945 order is over — officially.** When the leaders of Germany, France, and the United States all say so from the same stage, it is no longer a theory. Dalio's Big Cycle framework, once academic, is now the operating reality. The rules-based international system has given way to power politics.

2. **2026 rhymes with 1936, not 2008.** This is not a financial crisis that central banks can paper over with rate cuts. It is a structural shift in how the world is organized — involving debt, inequality, great-power rivalry, and democratic fragility simultaneously. The five types of "war" running in parallel compress what historically took a decade into years.

3. **Cycles end, but they also begin.** Every Stage 6 in history has eventually produced a Stage 1 — a new order built on the rubble of the old. The question is not whether a new system will emerge, but how painful the transition will be. Position your portfolio and your mindset for a turbulent but ultimately transformative decade ahead.
